The visitor guided through these pages is warmly welcomed.

You have just read a garden-path sentence; you are also reading the website of someone who spends a lot of time thinking about how readers find their way through language. I am currently an MSc Cognitive Neuroscience student at Radboud University Nijmegen. Before joining Radboud, I completed an MSc in Psycholinguistics at the University of Edinburgh, where I developed my interest in reading, eye movements, and sentence comprehension.

Broadly, my research interests center on ambiguity resolution. More recently, I have also become increasingly interested in multimodal communication and cognitive neuroscience methods, including EEG, MEG, MRI, and transcranial ultrasound stimulation.
